## 4) Full Project Narrative

### 4.1 Project Design

#### A. Problem Definition and Rationale
[Applicant Name] experiences recurring process bottlenecks in SNAP application and recertification operations: high inquiry volume for case status, verification document processing delays, and limited client transparency regarding next required steps. These barriers affect timely processing, increase administrative burden, and create avoidable client friction.

SNAP Connect directly addresses these barriers through service redesign and technology modernization centered on client and worker usability.

#### B. Project Goals and SMART Objectives
**Goal 1: Improve client transparency and communication.**  
- Objective 1.1: By Month 24, implement applicant status tracking for at least [X%] of new applications.  
- Objective 1.2: By Month 30, reduce status-related inbound calls by [Y%] from baseline.

**Goal 2: Improve verification workflow speed and reliability.**  
- Objective 2.1: By Month 30, reduce average days from document receipt to case action by [Z%].  
- Objective 2.2: By Month 30, reduce duplicate document submissions by [A%].

**Goal 3: Improve timeliness and continuity.**  
- Objective 3.1: By Month 36, increase timely application processing rate from [Baseline %] to [Target %].  
- Objective 3.2: By Month 36, increase recertification completion rate from [Baseline %] to [Target %].

#### C. Solution Components
1. **Client Status Tracker:** Mobile-first interface with plain-language milestones, outstanding tasks, and due dates.  
2. **Secure Document Upload:** Authenticated upload channels, file-type controls, receipt confirmation, and case linking.  
3. **Reminder/Alert Engine:** Configurable SMS/email templates for verifications, interviews, and recertification milestones.  
4. **Staff Workflow Dashboard:** Queue management by due date/risk, workload visibility, escalation markers, and document verification pipeline views.  
5. **Performance Dashboard:** Operational and client-experience indicators for continuous quality improvement.

#### D. Human-Centered Design Approach
The project will implement structured co-design with SNAP participants, call-center/client service staff, and eligibility workers. Activities include journey mapping, usability testing rounds, language clarity testing, and accessibility verification with assistive technology users.

## 14) Data Privacy and PII Protection Section

SNAP Connect will implement privacy-by-design safeguards consistent with SNAP confidentiality and applicable federal/state requirements:

1. **Encryption:** Data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest using approved cryptographic standards.
2. **Role-Based Access Control:** Least-privilege permissions by job role with periodic recertification.
3. **Audit Logging:** Immutable logs for document access, status changes, and administrative actions.
4. **Minimum Necessary Access:** Data segmentation and policy controls to limit exposure.
5. **Secure Document Storage:** Protected repositories with retention/disposition rules aligned to policy.
6. **Workforce Training:** Initial and annual privacy/security training for staff and contractors.
7. **Confidentiality Compliance:** Adherence to SNAP data sharing and confidentiality requirements and state policy.
8. **Decision Governance:** No automated final eligibility determinations unless explicitly authorized by governing law/policy and approved through formal governance.
